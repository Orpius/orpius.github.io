What is the Orpius Platform?

Orpius is a multi-agent software system designed to run long, multi-step tasks triggered by schedules or external events. It’s built for product teams adding agentic AI to their apps with minimal effort, and operations teams automating complex processes with AI agents that work alongside people.

Effortless & Secure Integration

Adding AI capabilities to your application through Orpius is fast, secure, and requires minimal code. Orpius isolates responsibilities across agents, creating a secure, structured way to access AI-driven features without compromising core systems.

Built for Work Not Assistance

Orpius isn’t about chat or summarization. It’s designed to execute non-interactive, multi-step tasks. Orpius agents act independently or with human collaboration to get the job done.

Built for What’s Next

Orpius is designed to evolve. As AI models improve, agents can take on more work autonomously. Its architecture makes it easy to extend with new capabilities so the system grows with your needs.

How it works

We’ve built a single, cohesive system that orchestrates AI agents, tools, data sources, human expertise, and logic to carry out complex, real-world tasks. Here’s how it works for operations teams.

Initialization

Start by defining outcomes, tasks, and when they should run, either on a schedule or in response to external events.

Task Assignment

The platform delegates tasks to both human and AI agent team members, while considering the capabilities of participants and your organisation’s oversight policies.

Execution

Agents work autonomously where possible and coordinate with humans when needed. For example, a triggered sensor can initiate a video stream analysis, prompt gate closure if needed, notify the appropriate staff, and log the entire event.

Iteration

Based on a definable schedule, the platform coordinates tasks autonomously without requiring constant human intervention.

Monitoring and Adjustment

Throughout a process, the Orpius platform monitors performance and task outcomes. If any deviations or inefficiencies are detected, the system dynamically adjusts the operations and reroutes task execution accordingly.
